About the Designer

About the Designer - Tsehaye Mehari

Who is Tsehaye Mehari?

Tsehaye Mehari is a skilled Ethiopian fashion designer who has spent more than two decades perfecting his craft. Born with an eye for detail and a talent for creativity, he began designing professionally and quickly became known for his clean stitching, unique patterns, and high-quality finishing.

He specializes in creating all types of Habesha clothing, including modern kemis, cultural dresses, wedding gowns, and simple everyday wear. His work is deeply connected to Ethiopian identity, and he takes pride in designing pieces that make women feel elegant, confident, and connected to their heritage. With a strong commitment to craftsmanship and customer satisfaction, Tsehaye continues to grow his brand and serve as an inspiring figure in the Habesha fashion industry.

History of Habesha Dress

History of Habesha Dress

Origin

The Habesha dress, also known as Habesha Kemis, has been an important part of Ethiopian and Eritrean culture for centuries. Traditionally made from handwoven cotton called shemma, the dress was created by skilled weavers who used simple wooden looms to produce long, soft, white fabric. Early Habesha dresses were usually plain white with delicate woven borders called tibeb, which often represented the wearerâ€™s region or community. The dress has always been worn during special occasions such as holidays, weddings, church ceremonies, and cultural celebrations, making it a strong symbol of identity, purity, and tradition



Modern Influence

In recent years, the Habesha dress has evolved while still keeping its cultural roots. Modern designers now experiment with new colors, patterns, bold tibeb designs, and fitted silhouettes. Many incorporate contemporary styles such as layered skirts, modern necklines, embroidery, and creative combinations of traditional and modern fabrics. Today, the Habesha dress is popular not only in Ethiopia but around the world, worn proudly at cultural events and showcased by the global Ethiopian diaspora. This blend of tradition and modern innovation helps the Habesha dress stay timeless, stylish, and meaningful to new generations.
